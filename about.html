Maker

I chose Traveling Photo classification as the research topic of my individual graduation thesis, with the aim of seeking practical implication of machine learning. I built dataset in various scales, and experimented classification efficiency and accuracy on different neural network structures, based on ConvJS library. This is the first time I touched machine learning, I learnt to organize information collected from wide resources, and how to design convincing experiment from my supervisor.
As an award of IoT Challenge, we received a box of GrovePi + Raspberry Pi. I brought them home and made some interesting experiments with my TV as monitor;) My initial idea was to sense the movements of an object, and play different tones by speaker, to mock a scene that puppet dancing alone music. However, I neglect the fact that GrovePi native speaker is not supported on Raspberry, so I used downloaded mp3 music pieces instead.
Out of interests, I followed online course -- Control of Mobile Robots on Coursera. It took me some struggling time to correlate calculus in math to mobile machine. But in the end, when I managed to implement a navigation system in Matlab stimulator, I enjoyed so much seeing my little red robot dodging obstacles and arriving target smoothly. I also got certificate of this course by Georgia Tech
In one of team projects, we used UI5 Gantt Chart control to visualize functions of Dock Appointment Scheduling. Warehouse manager needs to manage time schedule and book doors for shipping car arriving warehouse. We offered function of drag and drop to arrange schedule, visualization of overlapping and busy slot, and zoom in/out for easy planning.
